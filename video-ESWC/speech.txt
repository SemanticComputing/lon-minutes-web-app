[[slnc 2000]]
I will introduce the League of Nations Sampo web portal, build on the linked Open Data Service

# page 1+1
[[slnc 1500]]
This is the entry page of the portal. 
From here the user can choose between four different perspective views to the data;
the speeches, the people, the places, and the minute records. 

# page 2+1 
[[slnc 1000]]
Choosing the speeches perspective opens a semantic view to the eleven thousend and six hundred speeches held at the League of Nations between the years nineteen twenty and nineteen fourty six. 

The facets on the left allow the user to filter the results by some chosen criteria,
and the result table on the right gets automatically updated. 
[[slnc 600]]
The speeches can be limited to a specific speaker, 
[[slnc 600]]
by the language used in the speech, which are English or French in the data.
[[slnc 600]]
by the represented country,
[[slnc 600]]
by the gender of the speaker,
[[slnc 600]]
by the time of the speech,
[[slnc 600]]
or by the content, like for instance, when some specific location or topic is mentioned.

[[slnc 1500]]
In addition to the main result table, the other tabs in the speeches perspective allow the used to view the results as, like for instance, 
Pie or column charts,
[[slnc 600]]
Time series showing the most active speakers,
[[slnc 600]]
or as a visualization on a map.
[[slnc 200]]
where the user can zoom in and out, 
[[slnc 1500]]
and clicking on a marker opens up a pop up window showing a list of all speeches 
where the particular location was mentioned.
[[slnc 2000]]
# https://minutes.ldf.fi/en/speeches/page/s1935010653_fr/table
Among these speeches is the one held by Ethiopian Emperor Haile Selassie on the tenth of October in nineteen thirty five
[[slnc 1000]] 
At this instance page of a speech, you can see the entire content of the speech, 
and, in addition the time when the speech was held and well as lists of the people, locations, organizations, and general concepts mentioned in the speech.

# People perspective
[[slnc 1500]]
In addition to the speeches, from the main page the user can the user can choose to browse the people data in the League of nations Sampo. 
The person data contains biographical information of altogether three thousend three hundred people linked to the League of Nations minutes. 
[[slnc 1500]]
The people information includes like for instance the dates and places of their birth and death which is enriched from external sources such as Wikidata or Lonsea project. 
[[slnc 1500]]
On the perspective the user can filter the results based on their represented country, gender, or occupation.
[[slnc 2000]]
# https://minutes.ldf.fi/en/people/faceted-search/top-references
The other tabs in the person perspective allows the user to study, like for instance, who where the most referenced people in the speeches, 
[[slnc 2000]]
# https://minutes.ldf.fi/en/people/faceted-search/migrations
and a migration map showing arches connecting the places of birth and death,
[[slnc 2500]]
# https://minutes.ldf.fi/en/people/page/p1789044088392048865/table
The instance page of a person shows the biographical information, the related occupations, and a listing of the different roles in the organizations in the League of Nations.
[[slnc 1500]]
The second tab on the instance page lists all the speeches by the particular person, as well as the speeches mentioning her or him. 
[[slnc 1500]]
# https://minutes.ldf.fi/en/people/page/p1789044088392048865/person-network
The egocentric network is built based on the person references in the speeches.

[[slnc 2000]]
# https://minutes.ldf.fi/en/places/faceted-search/table?page=0
The places perspective shows all the locations in the dataset. 

[[slnc 1500]]
# https://minutes.ldf.fi/en/places/page/Q71/speeches-and-references
On the instance page you can see all the speeches and minutes mentioning the particular location. 
[[slnc 1500]]
The map on the instance page shows the location, as well as places within the main location. 

[[slnc 2000]]
The fourth data perspective, minutes, allows the user to browse all the records from the League of Nations minutes.

[[slnc 1500]]
In addition to reading the texts, you can, like for instance, view the most mentioned location of each year as a heatmap.

[[slnc 1500]]
